Orphan Drug Econometrics
Independent

Goal: Attempt to empirically evaluate whether orphan drug incentives are causally-enabling or strategically post-hoc for precision medicines in oncology.

The Orphan Drug Act of 1983 addressed a market failure in rare disease drug discovery. In addition to clinical trial tax credits and waived FDA fees, approved orphan drugs receive seven years of market exclusivity to recoupe expensive development costs for drugs targeting small patient populations. Precision medicines that target genetic subtypes of common diseases (i.e breast cancer) now often qualify for orphan drug designation, in spite of no clear market failure necesitating the incentives. This project examines whether the ODA's financial incentives were necessary for the development of precision oncology medicines, or whether industry investment had already been committed before orphan designation was ever sought.

    Key Findings:

  • 92% of precision oncology drugs with orphan drug designation received their designation after Phase II had already begun (median lag of +18 months post-Phase II initiation), versus only 37% of "true" orphan drugs for rare diseases (median lag of -2 months).
  • 84% of tracked precision oncology drugs with orphan designation expanded from their initial orphan indication to broad non-orphan populations within a median of 2.1 years post-approval, with 70% of supplemental approvals targeting non-orphan indications.

SudokuImputer: A Best-in-Class Graph Framework for MNAR/MAR Missing Data
Independent

Goal: Build a graphical framework to determine an optimized sequence of columns to impute via model-based imputations.

  • The design inspiration: In sudoku, the sequence and initialization of your previous guesses influence your subsequent guesses to solve the puzzle. Can we leverage sudoku-solving game theory to identify the sequence of columns maximally informative to impute a dataset with missing data?
  • Logistic regression-based imputation in the SudokuImputer framework outperforms state-of-the-art imputation strategies of varied complexity across numerous tabular dataset benchmarks.

Clinical scribe AI
MD+ Datathon, 2024

Goal: Build and benchmark a deterministic rules-based algorithm for chief complaint detection in patient-physician conversations.

  • Leveraged lingustic rules and conversational archetypes from outpatient visit transcripts (Aci-bench: Microsoft, Health AI) to build a detection algorithm for chief medical complaints.
  • In a benchmark against zero-shot language models, our non-AI algorithm demonstrated stronger detection performance than language models as scored by three independent physician reviewers.
